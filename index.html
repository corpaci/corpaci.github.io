Luiza Corpaci

I build systems that translate between levels of abstraction — currently an LLM pipeline at AMD that takes natural language specs and compresses them into formal verification assertions. The interesting part isn't that it works; it's characterizing where it stops working and why. That boundary is where most of my research lives.

If you care about: what formalization can't capture, why your interpretability probe tells you as much about itself as about the model, or whether there's an endogenous precision-weighting mechanism that separates living systems from merely active ones — you're in the right place.

I'm an AI engineer who works on teaching computers to check their own homework. At AMD, I build tools that turn plain English descriptions of how a chip should behave into formal proofs that it actually does. I'm also a researcher interested in a big question: when we try to make fuzzy, human ideas precise and checkable, what necessarily gets lost in translation?
