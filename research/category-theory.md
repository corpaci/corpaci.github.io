Category theory is the mathematics of structure-preserving maps. What draws me to it is that the same abstract patterns — functors, natural transformations, adjunctions — keep appearing across domains that look unrelated on the surface: logic, computation, linguistics, learning.

For AI research specifically, categorical frameworks offer a way to be precise about structure without committing to a particular representation — which matters when you're trying to say something general about what models can and cannot compute, or about how meaning relates to form.

This is a background interest that threads through the formalization and interpretability work rather than a standalone research thread.
