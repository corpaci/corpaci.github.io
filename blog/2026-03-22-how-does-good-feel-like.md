Does dinner with mom feel like a *good* use of your time? Is it good enough to anchor whatever it is you're doing instead against it?

<div class="day-only" markdown="1">
Time is finite. The people we love won't be here forever. This is an accessible truth we (probably) can agree on.[^finite-time]

Good feels like the choice we'd still make knowing the full weight of what we're giving up. he felt decision, not the optimied one. When we're present at dinner with mom, we're not calculating opportunity costs. We're in the moment that, when it's gone, we cannot recover through any achievement. Good feels like attending to what's irreplaceable while it's still here.

[^finite-time]: Maybe this will not be true for all of human existence, but it seems to be a limitation of today's experience. Until we reach whatever form of life expansion we figure out, I'll try to deal with the feebleness of being a biological human.

</div>

<div class="night-only" markdown="1">
We sometimes don't know what good feels like. It's not uncommon for people to misread their internals. 
Imagine the internals of the human deciding to rename the Department of Defense to the Department of war (yes, it is a thing; check war.gov). I'd bet some misfiring happened there. 

</div>

AI made idea generation nearly free, but we haven't yet scaled how we evaluate these ideas. We can check if something works correctly, but not if it's the right thing to build, nor if it's the good thing to build.

Moreover, human intelligence is no longer the center of the universe. Maybe that changed this year or this decade or this century. Maybe not everyone will agree as to when, but it's here and it's real and it fails often enough to negate its existence, but it also surprises often enough to prove its existence.

Jakob von Uexküll's concept (1909) describes how every organism has its own Umwelt, its subjective perceptual universe, shaped by its sensory apparatus and needs. Not "the world as it is," but "the world as perceived through this body, these sensors, these purposes."


<div class="night-only" markdown="1">
This made me think that mechanisticfeatures might be projecting an Umwelt onto the model's representation space. When features fracture, maybe it's because we're formalizing our own perceptual categories, not discovering the model's actual Umwelt.
</div>


<div class="beyond-only" markdown="1">
Can we build tools that help humans make trade-offs better? Or does quantifying them destroy the thing we're trying to protect? If we had an AI that could predict E[remaining dinners with mom], would using it help us make more meanigful choices or just make us more anxious? 

What if the AI's predictions were wrong? 

What if it was right but we couldn't verify its accuracy? 

What if it was right but we couldn't validate that it was the right thing to build in the first place?

Can we build tools to verify accross Umwelts? What if "alignment" is trying to make AI share human Umwelt, but that's structurally impossible, like teaching a tick to see colors?
</div>
